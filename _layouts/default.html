All Saints in Wighton was rebuilt in the 15th century, but parts of it are much older. A recent study of the fabric and the documents that survive has revealed it to be one of the most important churches in North Norfolk, and uncovered many fascinating details about its history, and the people who helped to build and conserve it.

The timeline below shows the main events over the last 1,000 years, including the rebuilding of the nave in 1417. To mark the 600th anniversary of the "new" nave, the village has launched a major restoration campaign to replace the roof and secure the future of the church for generations to come.
